How It Works

The listing process is very simple and we will guide you through it. Below is a step-by-step guide on the listing process.

Step 1

Firstly, we will ask you about the basic details of your property; the address, the number of bedrooms, what condition your property is in, etc. This step will be broken down into several simple steps:

  • Address
  • General (including number of floors, number of bedrooms etc)
  • Outdoor spaces
  • Parking
  • Heating (such as central heating and eco-friendly features)

Step 2

The next step will be allow you to give us as much information about each room of your house as you like. You really aught to give each room a name (such as “Master Bedroom”, “Kitchen” or “Utility Room”) and we suggest providing measurements, a brief description and up to four images.

We suggest having the measurements of your key rooms (especially rooms that are particularly large) before starting the wizard, but you can always save your progress as you go.

When it comes to writing a description we suggest being as brief as possible and listing key features of rooms. Features worth mentioning are fireplaces, bay windows and whether a bedroom is single or double.

For advice on how to take the best pictures for each room click here.

Step 3

The next section allows you to be more flexible with what you want to say about your property; after all you know your house better than anyone else!

Some aspects of the property we suggest discussing are era (such as Victorian/Pre-war/New Build/etc), open-plan, high-ceilings and any other interesting properties of your home.

Step 4

This section will allow you to review your listing and ensure that you have mentioned everything important about your property. It is not critical to mention every detail of your property but the more you mention generally the more interest your property will receive.

This is the last step that you will be able to edit your listing; however, you will be able edit your listing after it has gone live on our listing websites.
